About Me

About Me

I have been teaching yoga and training yoga teachers since the mid 90’s with a good dose of meditation and a bodywork practice thrown in. Over the years, as a one-to-one yoga teacher my practice inevitably became more therapeutic with a growing interest in the connections between what was manifesting physically the body of the client and what the client wanted to talk about in the session. This led me to the work of Nick Totton and Allison Preistman and a four year training in Embodied Relational Therapy, a form of body psychotherapy. The approach brought more of a joined-up sense of the body mind continuum and continues to endlessly inform and reflect the systemic nature of our being.

Why Yoga

Why Yoga

There are lots of definitions and preconceptions about what yoga is (and isn’t)! I like to think of and to share yoga as a mental and physical practice which can help anyone who wants to regain or to maintain natural, healthy movement patterns in their muscles and joints.

So, while it can be fun to see people doing headstands and seemingly impossible contortions, for me yoga at fifty is about simpler (and mostly enjoyable) poses and movements, often focused on the spine, neck, shoulders, hips or legs. This type of yoga practice then helps me to walk up hills and over stiles, to play tennis, to reach for things on the top shelf and to sleep comfortably at night !

And - while I don’t want to put you off with buzzwords ! - yoga is also a great practice for mindfulness and to steady and regulate the breath.

Over the past 15 years of regular yoga practice, I have been fortunate enough to try lots different styles with many fantastic teachers in London and elsewhere (particular thanks to Kate Ellis, Catherine Annis and Aki Omori for their guidance and support during my 2012-2014 Teacher Training at Triyoga in London).
